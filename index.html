GROVOLUT STUDIO ACADEMY
Complete Guide to Every Tool
Comprehensive instruction manuals for every tab in the Grovolut Growth Studio. Learn exactly how to use each tool, step by step, with pro tips and best practices from the Revolut creative team.
🎨
Asset Generator
Create campaign-ready creative assets from detailed prompts
What It Does
The Asset Generator is your primary creative production tool. It takes a detailed creative brief and generates visual assets — banners, video scripts, social posts, and ad creatives — tailored to Revolut's brand guidelines. Every output is auto-checked for brand compliance and can be localised into multiple markets.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Write your Creative Brief
In the text area under "Creative Brief / Prompt Description", type or paste your campaign idea. Be as specific as possible — include the product, target audience, platform, and desired outcome. Example: "Create a 6-second vertical video ad for Revolut Premium metal card launch in Spain, targeting millennials on Instagram Reels."
2
Click "Generate Asset"
Hit the green Generate Asset button. The AI will process your brief and produce a simulated asset preview in the Output Preview panel on the right. In prototype mode, this shows a simulated result — with a live API, it would generate real images/video.
3
Choose Localisation
Use the country flag buttons (🇪🇸 🇬🇧 🇫🇷 🇩🇪 🇵🇹 🇮🇹) below the brief to auto-adapt your asset for different markets. This adjusts language, currency formatting, regulatory disclaimers, and culturally relevant imagery.
4
Review the Output Preview
The right panel shows your generated asset with brand compliance badges. Green badges mean you're clear; orange/red means adjustments are needed. Check the AI-generated copy, visual description, and compliance status before exporting.
5
Export or Iterate
If the result looks good, export the asset. If not, refine your brief and regenerate. Tip: Use the Prompt Generator to create a more detailed brief first, then paste it here for better results.
💡 Pro Tips
Always start with a master prompt from the Prompt Generator — it dramatically improves output quality.
Use the localisation flags after generating your base asset to create variants efficiently.
Be specific about dimensions: mention "9:16 vertical" for Stories or "1:1 square" for feed posts.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Vague briefs like "make something cool for Revolut" give poor results. Be specific about product, audience, and platform.
Skipping the compliance check — always verify the badges before publishing to avoid regulatory issues.
🧠
Prompt Generator
Transform rough ideas into powerful, detailed master prompts
What It Does
The Prompt Generator is your idea enhancer. You write a rough, unpolished campaign idea — and it transforms it into a comprehensive, expert-level master prompt packed with visual direction, format guidance, audience targeting, and quality standards. You then copy that master prompt and paste it into any other tool (Asset Generator, Creator Tool, A/B Test Studio) for dramatically better results.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Write Your Rough Idea
In the "Your Rough Idea" textarea, describe your campaign concept in plain language. Don't worry about perfection — short phrases, bullet points, or stream-of-consciousness all work. Example: "metal card promo video for Spain millennials on TikTok"
2
Add Quick Tags (Optional)
Click any of the Quick Tags below the textarea to append context keywords. Tags like "Revolut Premium", "Spain Launch", or "TikTok Ad" help the AI understand your intent and produce more relevant sections in the master prompt.
3
Click "GENERATE MASTER PROMPT"
Hit the big teal button. The system analyses your rough idea, detects context (video vs. static, Spain vs. UK, premium vs. B2B, etc.) and assembles a comprehensive master prompt with sections for Visual Direction, Format Guidance, Audience & Tone, Localisation, and Quality Standards.
4
Review & Copy the Master Prompt
The generated prompt appears in a teal-bordered output card. Read through it, then click 📋 Copy Prompt to copy to clipboard. You can also use the "USE THIS PROMPT IN" shortcut buttons to jump directly to Asset Generator, Creator Tool, or A/B Test Studio with the prompt on your clipboard.
5
Save to Library
Click 💾 Save to Library to store the prompt in your Saved Prompts Library (right panel). Saved prompts persist across sessions, can be loaded, copied, or deleted at any time. You can store up to 50 prompts.
💡 Pro Tips
The more specific your rough idea, the better the master prompt. Mention specific products, audiences, platforms, and goals.
Save your best-performing prompts as templates — load and tweak them for new campaigns rather than starting from scratch.
Use the master prompt across multiple tools: paste into Asset Generator for visuals, Creator Tool for copy, and A/B Test for variants.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Writing the master prompt yourself instead of using this tool — the AI adds critical structure and sections you'd likely forget.
Forgetting to save good prompts — once you regenerate, the previous one is gone unless saved.
✂️
Creator Tool
Edit, refine, and assemble campaign creatives hands-on
What It Does
The Creator Tool is your hands-on editing workspace. Unlike the Asset Generator (which auto-produces from a brief), the Creator Tool gives you direct control over individual creative elements — headlines, body copy, CTAs, colour schemes, and layout. Think of it as Figma meets Canva, purpose-built for Revolut campaigns.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Start with a Template or Blank Canvas
Choose from pre-built Revolut templates (banner, story, carousel) or start with a blank canvas. Templates come pre-loaded with brand-approved fonts, colours, and layouts.
2
Edit Creative Elements
Use the editing panels to modify headlines, subtext, CTA buttons, and background visuals. Each element has real-time preview so you can see changes instantly. Paste a master prompt from the Prompt Generator to pre-fill intelligent content.
3
Preview & Export
Use the live preview panel to check how your creative looks across different sizes and platforms. When satisfied, export in the required format and resolution.
💡 Pro Tips
Paste master prompts from the Prompt Generator into the Creator Tool's brief field to get intelligent auto-suggestions for headlines and CTAs.
Always preview on mobile dimensions first — 80%+ of Revolut's audience views on mobile devices.

Inspiration Gallery
Browse proven campaign templates and remix winning creatives
What It Does
A curated library of past Revolut campaigns, competitor benchmarks, and industry-leading creative examples. Browse by category (video, static, stories, market), search by keyword, and one-click remix any template directly into the Asset Generator or Creator Tool.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Filter by Category
Use the filter buttons at the top (All, Video, Static, Stories, Spain, Web3, Metal Cards) to narrow down the gallery to relevant campaign types. You can also type keywords in the search box to find specific assets.
2
Preview & Analyse
Hover over any gallery card to reveal action buttons. Each card shows the campaign title, format tags (e.g., "6s animation", "Spain localised"), and performance indicators. Study what made successful campaigns work before creating your own.
3
Remix or Use in Campaign
Click Remix to load the template into the Creator Tool for customisation, or Use in Campaign to add it directly to an active campaign in the Campaign Library.
💡 Pro Tips
Sort by "Most Used" to see which templates your team relies on — these are proven performers.
Use the Spain and Web3 filters when creating market-specific campaigns to see localised examples.
Gallery starts with demo examples (hardcoded). Generate in Asset Generator → click "Add to Inspiration Gallery". Or use "Save Visible to Campaign Library" to move them into the real Campaign Library tab.
🎙️
Voice & Tone Lab
Define and test brand voice consistency across campaigns
What It Does
The Voice & Tone Lab lets you define, test, and enforce Revolut's brand voice across all creative output. Write sample copy, select a tone profile (Confident, Playful, Authoritative, Friendly), and see real-time scoring on how well it matches the brand guidelines. Use it to train new team members or validate copy before it goes live.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Select a Tone Profile
Choose from pre-defined tone profiles: Confident (default for Revolut), Playful (Gen Z campaigns), Authoritative (B2B/enterprise), or Friendly (onboarding flows). Each profile sets different scoring parameters.
2
Write or Paste Your Copy
Enter the headline, tagline, or body copy you want to test in the input field. The lab analyses word choice, sentence length, emotional tone, and brand vocabulary alignment.
3
Review the Score & Suggestions
The tone meter shows a score out of 100. Below it, you'll see specific suggestions: words to replace, phrases to tighten, and alternatives that better match the chosen tone profile. Aim for 80+ in all campaigns.
4
Listen to the Voice
Use the 🔊 Listen to Suggestion and 🔊 Play Input buttons to hear the text spoken using your browser's built-in voices. Select different voices from the dropdown. Double-click any tone preset button for a quick audio preview. Speech parameters (speed/pitch) adapt to the current tone sliders.
💡 Pro Tips
Test your copy against multiple tone profiles — what sounds "Confident" in English might read as "Aggressive" when localised to Spanish.
Use the lab to A/B test headlines before sending them to the A/B Test Studio for performance validation.
Click 🔊 buttons to hear how the copy actually *sounds*. Different browser voices (especially Spanish ones) give realistic previews for tone validation.

A/B Test Studio
Create side-by-side creative variants and predict winners
What It Does
The A/B Test Studio lets you create two or more creative variants side by side, compare them visually, and run AI-predicted performance analysis. It simulates click-through rates, engagement scores, and audience preference before you spend a single penny on media spend. Use it to validate creative decisions with data, not guesswork.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Create Variant A
Enter your first creative brief, headline, or visual concept in the "Variant A" panel. This is your control — usually the safer, proven approach.
2
Create Variant B
Enter your experimental creative in "Variant B". This could be a different headline, colour scheme, CTA wording, or entirely different visual approach. The more different it is from A, the more valuable the test.
3
Run the Comparison
Click Run A/B Test to get AI-predicted performance metrics for both variants. Review predicted CTR, engagement, and brand alignment scores. The system highlights the winning variant with a confidence percentage.
💡 Pro Tips
Test only one variable at a time (headline OR image OR CTA) — testing multiple changes makes it impossible to know what drove the result.
Paste master prompts from the Prompt Generator into both variants, tweaking just the key element you want to test.

Campaign Library
Organise, track, and manage all your campaign projects
What It Does
The Campaign Library is your central hub for organising all campaign projects. Every asset, brief, test result, and approval lives here. Create campaigns, add assets from other tools, track status (Draft → In Review → Approved → Live), and manage the full lifecycle from ideation to launch.
How to Use It
1
Create a New Campaign
Click "New Campaign" and give it a name, target market, and launch date. This creates a container folder for all related assets and briefs.
2
Add Assets
From the Asset Generator or Creator Tool, use the "Add to Campaign" option to attach creatives. You can also drag assets from the Inspiration Gallery.
3
Track Status & Approvals
Each campaign card shows its current status. Move campaigns through the pipeline as they progress. Stakeholders can approve or request changes directly within the library.

Content Calendar
Schedule and visualise your campaign timeline
What It Does
A visual scheduling tool that maps your campaigns across a timeline. See when assets are due, when campaigns launch, and identify gaps or overlaps in your content schedule. Integrates with Campaign Library so everything stays synchronised.
How to Use It
1
Navigate the Calendar
Use the month/week toggle to switch views. Click any date cell to see scheduled campaigns for that day. Colour-coded dots indicate campaign status (green = live, blue = scheduled, orange = draft).
2
Schedule Content
Click an empty date to create a new scheduled post or campaign launch. Set the time, platform, target market, and link the associated assets from your Campaign Library.
3
Drag to Reschedule
Drag campaign blocks between dates to reschedule. The calendar automatically warns you about conflicts (e.g., two Spain campaigns launching on the same day).

Analytics & Insights
Track campaign performance and creative effectiveness
What It Does
Real-time performance dashboards showing KPIs across all campaigns: impressions, CTR, conversion rates, brand lift, and cost-per-acquisition. AI-powered insights surface what's working and what needs attention, so you can optimise spend and creative strategy on the fly.
How to Use It
1
View Dashboard KPIs
The top row shows headline metrics (Total Reach, Avg CTR, Conversion Rate, Active Campaigns). These update in real-time as campaign data flows in.
2
Drill into Charts
Click on any chart to drill down into specific campaigns, markets, or time periods. Compare performance across Spain vs. UK, or Video vs. Static formats.
3
Act on AI Insights
The AI Insight cards at the bottom highlight anomalies and opportunities: "Spain video CTR is 24% above average — consider increasing budget" or "UK carousel engagement dropped 12% this week."

Target Markets • Focus Mode
Hyper-local campaigns for Revolut's 40+ current markets + 100-country expansion pipeline
What It Does
The Target Markets tab is your command center for hyper-local advertising campaigns. It allows you to explore details for Revolut's 40+ active countries and expansion pipeline, lock the entire Growth Studio environment to a specific local context using Focus Mode, and generate detailed local campaign briefs instantly.
Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Explore & Filter Markets
Browse the country cards. You can search by name, code, continent, or language, and filter by Tier (Tier 1 Core, Tier 2 Expansion, Tier 3 Pipeline). Sort by priority, customer base, YoY growth, or alphabetically.
2
Activate Focus Mode
Click 🎯 Activate Focus Mode on any market card. A green banner will appear at the top of the Growth Studio, locking all other creative tools (Asset Generator, AI Agents, etc.) to that country. All generations and compliance checks will auto-adapt to that market's regulations, language, and culture.
3
Open the AI Brief Generator
Click the 📝 Brief button on a market card to automatically select it in the AI Campaign Brief Generator section at the bottom, or select a country manually using the dropdown list.
4
Select Product & Objective
Select which Revolut product you want to promote (e.g., Premium, Metal, Ultra, Business, Crypto, eSIM) and your marketing objective (e.g., Acquisition, Activation, Upsell, Brand Awareness).
5
Generate and Review Brief
Click 🤖 Generate Campaign Brief. The AI will output a fully localised brief containing key target audience insights, local regulatory warnings (e.g., Spain GdE rules, UK FCA requirements), translation guidelines, and recommended local angles.
💡 Pro Tips
Deactivating Focus Mode: Click the ✕ Exit Market Mode button in the top green banner to return to the global context at any time.
Use the AI-generated brief parameters as prompt inputs in the Asset Generator to produce highly customized local creatives.
Pay close attention to the regulatory indicator badge (✓ Approved, ⏳ In Progress, ⏸ Pending) to ensure local campaigns have greenlit compliance.

Manage team members, roles, and collaboration
What It Does
The Team Workspace shows all active team members, their roles, current workload, and collaboration status. Assign assets to specific people, track who's working on what, and manage permissions (Editor, Reviewer, Admin). Integrated chat and commenting keeps feedback centralised.
How to Use It
1
View Team Members
See all team members with their roles, status (Online/Busy/Away), and current assignments. Click any member to view their profile and activity feed.
2
Assign Work

Collaborate in Real Time

Brand Guardian
Automated brand compliance and regulatory checks
What It Does
Brand Guardian is your automated compliance officer. It continuously scans all assets in the studio for brand guideline violations (wrong fonts, off-brand colours, missing logos), regulatory issues (missing FCA disclaimers, GdE requirements), and quality problems (low resolution, incorrect dimensions). Every issue gets a severity badge and a one-click fix suggestion.
How to Use It
1
Check the Dashboard
The main panel shows a compliance score (target: 95%+). Below it, flagged assets are listed with issue descriptions and severity levels (Critical, Warning, Info).
2
Review Flagged Items
Click any flagged item to see the exact violation, a preview of the issue, and the suggested fix. Common flags: "Missing ES financial disclaimer", "Logo too small", "Non-approved font detected."
3
Auto-Fix or Override
Use "Auto-Fix" for simple issues (add missing disclaimer, resize logo) or "Override" with a justification for intentional deviations. All overrides are logged for audit trails.

AI Agents
Autonomous AI workers that handle tasks in the background
What It Does
AI Agents are autonomous workers that run in the background. Each agent specialises in a task: Copy Agent writes ad copy, Visual Agent generates image concepts, Compliance Agent checks regulations, Localisation Agent translates and adapts content. You assign them tasks, they work independently, and notify you when done.
How to Use It
1
View Active Agents
The dashboard shows all available agents with their current status (Idle, Working, Queued). Each agent card displays its specialisation, current task, and completion progress.
2
Assign a Task
Click any idle agent and assign a campaign brief. The agent will begin processing immediately. You can queue multiple tasks — they execute in order and notify you on completion.
3
Review Agent Output
When an agent finishes, its output appears in the "Completed" queue. Review the generated content, approve or reject it, and optionally feed it into other tools (Asset Generator, Campaign Library).
